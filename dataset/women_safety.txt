SOURCE: Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023
TOPIC: Women's Safety and Sexual Offences

1. Deceitful Sexual Intercourse: It is a specific crime under BNS to employ deceitful means, such as making false promises of marriage, employment, or promotion, or concealing one's identity, to have sexual intercourse. 
2. Mob Lynching and Hate Crimes: Grievous hurt or murder by a group of five or more people based on sex, race, caste, or personal belief is punishable by life imprisonment or death.
3. Statement Recording: Statements of female victims of sexual offences must be recorded by a woman police officer or any woman officer. As far as practicable, the statement should be recorded by a woman Magistrate.
4. Audio-Video Recording: To ensure transparency and protect the victim, the recording of the victim's statement in sexual offence cases must be done through audio-video means.
5. Free Medical Treatment: All hospitals (public or private) are mandated to provide free first-aid and medical treatment to victims of crimes against women and children immediately.
6. Attendance at Police Station: A woman, a person under 15, or a person over 60 cannot be forced to attend a police station for interrogation. They must be questioned at their place of residence.
