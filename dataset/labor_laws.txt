SOURCE: The Four New Labour Codes of India
TOPIC: Worker Rights, Wages, and Occupational Safety

1. Minimum Wages: The Code on Wages ensures a statutory National Floor Level Minimum Wage. State governments cannot set minimum wages below this floor. All workers, regardless of sector, are entitled to minimum wages.
2. Timely Payment: Wages must be paid within specific timelines (e.g., daily workers at the end of the day; monthly workers by the 7th of the following month).
3. Overtime Pay: Work beyond regular daily/weekly limits mandates overtime pay at twice the normal wage rate (double wages).
4. Gender Parity: Employers are strictly prohibited from discriminating on the basis of gender in matters of wage payment and recruitment for the same or similar work.
5. Gig and Platform Workers: The Code on Social Security legally recognizes gig workers (e.g., delivery drivers, freelancers) and provides mechanisms for social security funds covering life/disability cover, health/maternity benefits, and old age protection.
6. Leave Encashment: Workers can carry forward annual leave and encash it. The threshold for earning leave is calculated based on working 180 days in a calendar year.
7. Safe Working Conditions: Employers are mandated to provide free annual health check-ups for employees of a certain age and maintain strict safety standards.
