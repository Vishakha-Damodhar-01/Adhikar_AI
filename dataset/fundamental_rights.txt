SOURCE: The Constitution of India (Part III)
TOPIC: Fundamental Rights of Citizens

1. Article 14 (Equality before law): The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.
2. Article 15 (Prohibition of discrimination): The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
3. Article 19 (Freedoms): Guarantees citizens the right to freedom of speech and expression, to assemble peacefully, to form associations, to move freely throughout India, and to practice any profession.
4. Article 21 (Protection of life and personal liberty): No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law. This includes the right to a speedy trial, the right to legal aid, and the right to privacy.
5. Article 22 (Protection against arrest and detention): Grants protection to persons who are arrested or detained, ensuring they are informed of the grounds of arrest, have the right to consult a legal practitioner, and must be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours.
6. Article 32 (Right to Constitutional Remedies): Allows individuals to approach the Supreme Court directly if they believe their fundamental rights have been violated, using writs like Habeas Corpus (to produce a detained person) or Mandamus (to command a public authority to perform its duty).
