SOURCE: Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023
TOPIC: Arrest Rights and Procedures in India

1. Right to know grounds of arrest: Every police officer arresting a person without a warrant must immediately communicate the full particulars of the offence and the grounds for arrest.
2. Right to inform a relative/friend: The police officer making the arrest must inform a designated relative, friend, or nominated person about the arrest and the place of detention immediately.
3. Right to legal representation: An arrested person has the right to consult an advocate of their choice during interrogation, though not throughout the entire interrogation.
4. Protection against unnecessary restraint: The arrested person shall not be subjected to more restraint than is necessary to prevent their escape. Handcuffs are only to be used for habitual, repeat offenders, or those accused of severe offences like terrorism or organized crime.
5. 24-Hour Rule: No police officer shall detain an arrested person in custody for a longer period than under all the circumstances of the case is reasonable. This period shall not exceed 24 hours, excluding the time necessary for the journey to the Magistrate's court.
6. Medical Examination: An arrested person has the right to be examined by a medical officer immediately after arrest to record any existing injuries, which protects against police brutality.
7. Arrest of Women: No woman shall be arrested after sunset and before sunrise. Under exceptional circumstances, a woman police officer must obtain prior permission from the Judicial Magistrate before making a night arrest.
